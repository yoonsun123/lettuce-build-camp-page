What Your Child Will Create:

This advanced, college-prep level camp teaches students how artificial intelligence actually works by training real machine learning models using professional data science tools. Students will create their own AI-powered "smart turkey" that must navigate through a dangerous pre-built obstacle course called "The Gauntlet" to escape becoming Thanksgiving dinner.
Each student trains their own personalized machine learning model that acts as their turkey's brain, making split-second survival decisions based on training data. Working with Python and industry-standard data science libraries, students will analyze historical survival data, identify patterns in successful escape strategies, and train classification models using Decision Trees and other algorithms. Their AI turkey must make intelligent decisions at each challenge: Should it hide from the farmer or run? Take the risky shortcut or the safe route? Trust the decoy turkey or go solo?
By the final day, students compete in a live championship where all their trained AI turkeys navigate "The Gauntlet" simultaneously. The models with the best training survive the longest, creating an exciting, competitive environment where students see their machine learning skills translate directly into game performance. Winners are determined by which turkeys make it furthest through the obstacle course‚Äîa direct measure of how well students trained their models.


Skills & Benefits:

Your child will gain genuine data science and machine learning experience that stands out on college applications and prepares them for STEM careers. They'll learn the complete ML workflow: exploratory data analysis, understanding training data patterns, feature engineering, model training with Decision Trees and ensemble methods, evaluation using accuracy metrics and confusion matrices, and real-world application integration.
This isn't a superficial "intro to AI" course‚Äîstudents work with Python libraries used by professional data scientists and understand core concepts like supervised learning, overfitting, classification, and model optimization. The competitive element teaches students to iterate and improve their models based on performance feedback, mirroring real-world data science workflows. Perfect for motivated students considering computer science, data science, or AI engineering majors who want hands-on experience beyond what high school typically offers.
